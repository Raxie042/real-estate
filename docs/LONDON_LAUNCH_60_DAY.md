# London-First Launch Playbook (60 Days)

## Objective
Launch commercially in London as the first prestige market while preserving global product positioning and architecture.

## Strategy
- Product remains global (multi-locale, multi-currency, international discovery)
- Commercial rollout is city-first (London)
- Build scarcity and prestige from day one (invitation-only supply)

## Commercial Model (London)

### Tiers
- Private Tier: USD 5,000–8,000/year
- Elite Tier: USD 15,000–30,000/year

### Packaging Principles
- Annual contracts by default
- Limited city inventory (cap by borough/segment)
- Add-ons for premium distribution:
  - Homepage spotlight
  - International newsletter feature
  - Social showcase campaign

### Scarcity Rules (London)
- Max active agencies in launch cohort: 25
- Max Elite agencies in launch cohort: 8–10
- Max homepage spotlight slots per month: 8
- Waitlist starts once cap is reached

## 60-Day Execution Timeline

## Phase 1: Days 1–14 (Foundation)

### GTM Setup
- Finalize London tier card and terms
- Define admission rubric and approval committee
- Create London landing section copy (luxury positioning)
- Prepare one-page sales deck and outbound email template

### Targeting
- Build list of top 80 London agencies (prime and super-prime focus)
- Prioritize by listing quality, brand fit, and international buyer orientation
- Segment into A-list (20), B-list (30), C-list (30)

### Operational Setup
- Assign one owner for admissions
- Assign one owner for commercial conversion
- Define SLA for agency response and onboarding

## Phase 2: Days 15–35 (Acquisition)

### Outreach Motion
- Warm intros first (founders/advisors/network)
- Direct outreach to decision-makers (agency principals/heads of growth)
- Weekly invite waves in limited batches (scarcity signal)

### Weekly Targets
- 30 qualified conversations/week
- 10 applications/week
- 6 admissions/week
- 3 paid conversions/week

### Messaging
- Sell exposure to international capital, not generic lead volume
- Emphasize curated competition and controlled inventory
- Reinforce invitation-only access and limited London cohort

## Phase 3: Days 36–60 (Conversion + Retention)

### Conversion Program
- Founder-led close calls for top agencies
- Offer launch-window benefit (non-discount, value-add)
- Fast onboarding track for accepted Elite agencies

### Retention Readiness
- 30-day performance review for each signed agency
- Visibility and lead quality recap
- Expansion path to Elite or add-ons where fit exists

## KPI Dashboard (Weekly)
- Pipeline:
  - Outreach sent
  - Meetings booked
  - Applications received
  - Admissions approved
- Revenue:
  - New annual contracts (count)
  - New ACV booked
  - Average contract value
- Quality:
  - Agency acceptance rate
  - Time-to-onboard
  - Lead quality score
- Scarcity:
  - Slots filled vs cap
  - Waitlist size

## 60-Day Targets
- 12–18 signed London agencies
- 4–7 Elite contracts
- ACV booked: USD 180k–420k (range depends on tier mix)
- Waitlist established with at least 10 qualified agencies

## Sales Assets Checklist
- London one-pager (positioning + cohort cap + pricing range)
- Tier matrix (Private vs Elite)
- Admissions criteria (external summary)
- FAQ (why invitation-only, why annual)
- Contract template + onboarding checklist

## Risk Controls
- Avoid discount-led selling (preserve premium signal)
- Do not exceed capacity caps early
- Reject low-fit agencies even if they can pay
- Maintain strict content quality standards for listings

## Expansion Trigger (Post-London)
Expand to next city only after all conditions are met:
- London cohort renewal intent > 70%
- Slot utilization > 80%
- Stable onboarding SLA
- Proven ACV consistency over 6–8 weeks
